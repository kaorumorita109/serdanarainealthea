Typhoon, on the other hand, is a tropical cyclone that develops between 180 degrees and 100 degrees E in the Northern Hemisphere. Oftentimes, people confuse between the terms hurricane and typhoon. According to the National Aeronautics and Space Administration or NASA, the terms hurricane and typhoon are regionally specific names for a strong tropical cyclone. In fact Hurricanes develop in the North Atlantic Ocean, while typhoons develop in the Northwest Pacific Ocean.
